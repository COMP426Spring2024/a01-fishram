Medusa

Medusa, one of the three Gorgons, was the only mortal among them. Her hair of living, venomous snakes and a gaze that turned anyone who looked into her eyes to stone made her a terrifying figure in Greek mythology.

Origin

Medusa was not always a monster. A curse placed upon her by Athena transformed Medusa's beautiful hair into serpents.

Encounter with Perseus

Perseus was sent on a quest to bring back the head of Medusa. Using his shield as a mirror, he managed to behead Medusa without looking directly at her.

Legacy

  • Medusa's head was used by Perseus to perform heroic deeds.
  • Even after her death, the image of Medusa's head had the power to ward off evil, symbolizing protection.
